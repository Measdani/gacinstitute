Our story

We built this out of two summers, twenty-five girls, and a county that kept calling us back.

This is not a bio page. It is the origin story of an institution that started with one cohort and became a talent pipeline for the people and employers of Atlanta.

How it started

The first summer, we taught code and digital fabrication to twenty-five girls who had never held a soldering iron. The second summer, a DeKalb county team asked for more, and we built a program that respected effort and earned access.

Today, we are designing pathways so that Atlanta youth and adults can move from curiosity to certification to career without asking permission for every step.

Vision

The building will feel like a lab for people who want to build, think, and take a break in a room designed for focus. Every badge, every tool, every aisle is part of a clear progression.

The vision

The building

Open workshop space, small team labs, and a front door that says this is a real place for real work.

The diagonal door

A symbolic entrance to a new path: earned access and earned progress, not a waiting list.

The badges

Each badge is a milestone. Each milestone unlocks tools, mentors, and paid opportunities.

The brain break room

A space for pause, play, and regrouping so the next project begins with energy, not burnout.

The earned access philosophy

We don’t give things away because respect is not a charity act — it is a structure. When students earn access, they build confidence, community trust, and a track record that employers can rely on.

This is how we protect our program from being a free-for-all and turn it into a credentialed pathway that actually leads somewhere.

The badge system

Click each level to see what it requires and what it unlocks.

Passport

Complete orientation, safety training, and your first team build. Unlock access to afterschool workshops.

Bronze

Finish the first project, attend mentoring sessions, and demonstrate teamwork. Unlock weekend labs.

Silver

Earn a skills assessment, complete digital resume work, and qualify for paid project roles.

Gold

Complete the advanced track, deliver a capstone project, and unlock employer introductions.

Platinum

Lead a team project, mentor newer participants, and prepare for certification testing.

Catalyst

Graduate with a portfolio, employer referral, and priority placement in workforce partner opportunities.

Leadership

Led by a DeKalb education partner with experience launching youth STEM programs, securing county funding, and delivering measurable outcomes.

The founder’s track record includes DeKalb summer bridge programs, workforce alignment meetings, and student pathways that move from classroom to job-ready skill.
